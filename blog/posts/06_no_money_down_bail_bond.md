## The Number One Question We Get

When someone calls about a bail bond, the first question is almost always: *"How much do I need right now?"*

We get it. Arrests don't happen at convenient times. You might not have $2,500 sitting in your bank account at 2:00 AM on a Wednesday. So the question becomes: **Can you get a bail bond with no money down in Florida?**

Here's the honest answer.

## Florida Law Sets the Premium Rate

In Florida, the **bail bond premium is regulated by the state**. For state charges, the premium is **10% of the total bail amount**. For federal charges, it's **15%**. These rates are set by the Florida Department of Financial Services and apply to every bail bond company in the state.

That means if bail is set at $10,000, the legal premium is $1,000. **No bail bond company can legally charge you less than this** — and any company that advertises "0% down bail bonds" should raise a red flag.

## So What Does "No Money Down" Actually Mean?

When you see "no money down" bail bond advertising, it typically means one of two things:

### 1. Payment Plans (The Legitimate Version)

Many bail bond companies — Shamrock included — offer **payment plans** that allow you to spread the premium over time. So while the total cost doesn't change, you might only need a portion upfront to get the bond posted.

**Example:**

- Bail amount: $10,000
- Premium: $1,000
- **Down payment**: $250–$500
- **Remaining balance**: paid in installments over 3–6 months

This is legal, common, and how most families handle larger bonds. The bail bond company takes on the financial risk of posting the full bond while you pay off the premium.

### 2. Collateral Instead of Cash (Less Common)

In some cases, a bail bond company may accept **collateral** — such as a vehicle title, jewelry, or property — in place of a cash down payment. The collateral is returned when the case is resolved and the bond is exonerated.

This is more common for very large bonds ($50,000+) and requires the collateral to have verifiable value.

## What to Watch Out For

Not every "no money down" offer is what it seems. Be cautious of:

- **Hidden fees**: Some companies add service charges, filing fees, or administrative costs on top of the premium
- **Extremely high interest on payment plans**: Make sure you understand the total cost, not just the monthly payment
- **Unlicensed agents**: Always verify that the bail bond company is licensed with the Florida Department of Financial Services
- **Pressure tactics**: A reputable bail bond company will never pressure you into signing before you understand the agreement
