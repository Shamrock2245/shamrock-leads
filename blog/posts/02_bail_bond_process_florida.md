## What Happens When Someone Gets Arrested in Florida?

When someone is arrested in Florida, the clock starts ticking. The booking process, the first appearance hearing, the bail amount — it can all feel overwhelming when you're on the outside trying to help. But here's the truth: **the bail bond process in Florida is straightforward once you understand the steps.**

At Shamrock Bail Bonds, we've helped thousands of families navigate this process across Southwest Florida. This guide walks you through every stage so you know exactly what to expect.

## Step 1: The Arrest and Booking

After an arrest, the defendant is transported to the county jail for **booking**. This process typically takes 2–6 hours and includes:

- **Fingerprinting and photographing** (mugshot)
- **Recording personal information** (name, date of birth, address)
- **Documenting the charges**
- **Entering the defendant into the jail system**

During booking, the defendant's information becomes available in the county's online jail roster — this is how bail bond agents identify new cases.

## Step 2: Bail is Set

Bail can be set in two ways:

- **By a bail schedule**: Many counties in Florida use a standard bail schedule that assigns a dollar amount based on the charge. For common misdemeanors and felonies, bail may be set automatically during booking.
- **By a judge at First Appearance**: For more serious charges, or when the bail schedule doesn't apply, a judge will set bail at the defendant's **First Appearance hearing**, which must happen within **24 hours** of arrest per Florida law.

The bail amount depends on factors like the severity of the charges, the defendant's criminal history, ties to the community, and flight risk.

## Step 3: Contacting a Bail Bondsman

Once bail is set, the defendant or their family can contact a licensed bail bond agent. Here's what you'll need:

- **The defendant's full legal name**
- **Date of birth**
- **The jail where they're being held**
- **The booking number** (if available)
- **The bail amount**

At Shamrock, we can usually find your loved one in our system before you even call — we monitor every booking in real time across Lee, Charlotte, Collier, DeSoto, Hendry, Manatee, and Sarasota counties.

## Step 4: Signing the Bond Paperwork

The **indemnitor** (also called the co-signer) signs the bail bond agreement and pays the **premium** — which is **10% of the total bail amount** for state charges in Florida.

For example, if bail is set at $10,000, the premium is $1,000.

**With Shamrock, the paperwork process is 100% digital.** We send all documents via text message or our secure client portal. You can sign from your phone in under 5 minutes — no office visit required.

## Step 5: The Bond is Posted

Once the paperwork is signed and the premium is paid, the bail bond agent **posts the bond** with the county jail. This notifies the jail that the defendant's bail has been secured.

Release times vary by county:

- **Lee County**: 4–8 hours after bond is posted
- **Charlotte County**: 2–4 hours
- **Collier County**: 4–6 hours
- **DeSoto County**: 2–4 hours

These times depend on jail staffing, the time of day, and how busy the facility is.

## Step 6: Release

The defendant is released with conditions. These typically include:

- **Appearing at all scheduled court dates**
- **Not getting re-arrested**
- **Staying within the jurisdiction** (unless the court says otherwise)
- **Checking in with the bail bond agent** as required

Missing a court date can result in a **bond forfeiture** and a warrant for the defendant's arrest.

## What Happens After Release?

The bail bond remains active until the case is **resolved** — whether by trial, plea deal, or dismissal. Once the case is closed, the bond is **exonerated** (released), and the bail bond agent's financial obligation to the court ends.

**The premium you paid is non-refundable** — it's the fee for the bail bond service, regardless of the case outcome.
